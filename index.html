Four Decades of Dancers

Pacific Dance has been training Orange County dancers for nearly forty years — family-owned and run by Tim & Lori since 1994. Working LA professionals teach here (including a Hamilton choreographer master class), Lori holds an MFA from UC Irvine, and alumni have gone on to Broadway, Cirque du Soleil, Disney, and professional companies.

And still — every dancer is welcome. Recreational through professional track, age 2 through adults in their 80s, all body types and ability levels, seven days a week.

The story · Who we're talking to

Who We're Talking To

The website's real reader is almost always a parent — but three audiences walk through the door, and every page should feel like it was written for them.

The decision-maker

Parents of young dancers

Looking for a safe, warm place where their kid will be seen and grow. They want proof of quality — and reassurance their child won't get lost in "the largest studio in the area."

The dancer

Kids & serious teens

From a first Mommy & Me class at age 2 to performing groups, competition, and PD Elite. Teens want energy, real training, and teachers with real credits.

The lifelong mover

Adults, 20s–80s

Keeping fit, coming back to dance, or starting for the first time. Classes seven days a week make it possible around real schedules.

🛡️

The #1 objection, answered everywhere: "largest" can read as "my kid will get lost." So the site always pairs scale with the human counterweight — small, capped classes and Lori's personal communication with parents.

Three Angles — Which One Leads?

All three are true, and all three will live on the site. This pick decides which one fronts the homepage, the ads, and the first sentence of every page.

Angle 1 · Belonging
"More than a studio. A second home."

Leads with community — the phrase your families already use unprompted. Warmest welcome for nervous first-time parents.

Watch-out: needs credentials nearby to read as serious.

Angle 2 · Joy → confidence
"Pure joy in dance. Confidence for life."

Leads with the outcome parents are really buying — and it's your own sentence from the discovery call.

Watch-out: "joy" must stay specific to Pacific Dance moments.

Angle 3 · Inclusive excellence
"Every dancer. Every level. One studio."

Leads with scale and credentials — the strongest differentiation against every other studio in the area.

Watch-out: "largest" needs the personal counterweight every time.

Our lean, if useful: Pure Joy leads, Rec-to-Pro carries the proof, Second Home warms the community moments. The two you don't pick become supporting threads, not cuts.

The story · How it sounds

How It Sounds

Whichever angle leads, the voice stays the same: Warm · Uplifting · Confident · Specific — a proud parent's voice with a professional's credentials. Never cutthroat, never generic.

✓ What it sounds like
  • Parent-directed and outcome-oriented — joy now, confidence for life
  • Specific to Pacific Dance: real moments, real names, real credits
  • Inclusive excellence — serious training without the dog-eat-dog
  • Personal and family-run: Tim & Lori are characters, not a corporation
✕ What it avoids
  • Generic kids-activity "joy" fluff that could be any studio
  • Competition-obsessed, elite-only "dance mom" energy
  • Unproven superlatives — every claim gets a receipt
  • Studio jargon parents don't speak (syllabus, adjudication, tilt)

Words we lean on

Words we retire

The site · The button · Question № 4

One Button, Everywhere — What Should It Say?

These are the most-clicked words on the site: every page, every ad, every post points at this one button. Four candidates — same offer, four different feelings.

Book a Free Class
Our lean · the appointment

"Book" makes it real — a spot with your name on it, not a maybe. The most commitment per click, and it sets up Lori's follow-up perfectly.

Try a Free Class
The gentle ask

Lowest pressure — "try" gives nervous parents an easy out. The trade: it can whisper "noncommittal" and invite no-shows.

Start with a Free Class
The journey

"Start" implies class one of many — enrollment-minded from the first click. Slightly longer on a phone screen.

Get a Free Class
The offer

Punchy and deal-flavored. Strong in ads — but on a premium studio's site it can read a little coupon-y.
